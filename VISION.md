# Vision

Work moves faster than the tools that track it. Email, Slack, meetings, tickets - every conversation creates tasks that vanish before anyone writes them down. Cowork Tasks watches the work happen and keeps a live kanban current. We are building the ambient layer between work and the record of work.

---

## The 5-year picture

By 2031, every AI-native team has a Cowork Tasks board. Not because they signed up - because the work that was already happening got tracked automatically the moment they connected their first tool. The kanban became the system of record for what's actually getting done, replacing the half-maintained Linear projects, the abandoned Asana boards, the Notion docs nobody updates.

The product is no longer just a kanban. It is a work intelligence layer. Cowork Tasks knows what every team member is working on, what's blocked, what's stale, what should be cancelled. Slack, email, and meetings become inputs. The board is the synthesized output.

The company is a $200M ARR vertical SaaS. Free for solo developers forever (MIT, local-first). Team plans for $15/user/month. Enterprise on-premise for compliance-heavy orgs. A platform API where third-party AI agents read and write the work graph - Stripe-shaped revenue from infrastructure usage, not seats.

The OSS repo has 100K stars. It is the default kanban citation in every "best dev tools 2031" listicle. Linear, Asana, and Trello still exist, but they are now considered legacy - the tools you used when you had to maintain your own boards.

The phrase "I have to update my tasks" is gone from the language of work.

## Anti-positioning (who Cowork Tasks is not for)

- Teams that want a manual project planning tool
- People who enjoy maintaining boards
- Anyone who needs Gantt charts, sprint velocity dashboards, or org-wide reporting
- Project managers who want to maintain the board

Cowork Tasks is for individuals and small teams who want the board to maintain itself.

## What we will not do

- Add features that require manual entry as the primary path
- Build a full project management tool (Gantt, sprints, capacity planning)
- Move to a closed-source business model on the core
- Sell user data
- Train models on user data without explicit opt-in
